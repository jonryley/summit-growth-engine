# Opportunity Classification

Classification determines the level of governance, not just the size of the deal.

It defines how much structure, scrutiny, and control is required for an opportunity to progress.

---

# Classification Levels

Lite → Standard → Strategic → Complex → Transformational

---

## 1. Lite

### Definition
Small, well understood opportunities with minimal commercial or delivery risk.

Typically repeat business, extensions, or known solutions.

### Characteristics
- Known customer  
- Clear requirement  
- Low value  
- Short sales cycle  
- Low delivery complexity  

### Governance Level
Low

### Gate Rigor
Light

### Controls
- Basic qualification  
- Minimal documentation  
- Limited review required  

### Review Cadence
Ad hoc or weekly

---

## 2. Standard

### Definition
Well defined opportunities with moderate value and manageable risk.

Represents core day-to-day business activity.

### Characteristics
- Defined scope  
- Competitive environment  
- Moderate value  
- Some stakeholder complexity  

### Governance Level
Medium

### Gate Rigor
Structured

### Controls
- Full stage progression  
- Standard gate checks  
- Defined opportunity fields  
- Basic commercial validation  

### Review Cadence
Weekly

---

## 3. Strategic

### Definition
Opportunities that are important to growth, key accounts, or market positioning.

Success influences future pipeline and customer access.

### Characteristics
- Key customer or account  
- High value or visibility  
- Strong competition  
- Cross-functional involvement  

### Governance Level
High

### Gate Rigor
Strong

### Controls
- Formal gate reviews  
- Leadership visibility  
- Clear value case  
- Defined win strategy  

### Review Cadence
Weekly + Monthly leadership review

---

## 4. Complex

### Definition
High complexity opportunities with significant delivery, commercial or stakeholder risk.

Requires deeper planning, coordination, and governance.

### Characteristics
- Multiple stakeholders  
- Unclear or evolving requirements  
- High delivery risk  
- Commercial complexity  
- Longer sales cycle  

### Governance Level
Very High

### Gate Rigor
Rigorous

### Controls
- Formal gate packs  
- Risk and mitigation planning  
- Cross-functional alignment  
- Delivery readiness checks  
- Scenario planning  

### Review Cadence
Weekly + Monthly + Escalation reviews

---

## 5. Transformational

### Definition
Opportunities that materially change the organisation’s growth trajectory, capability, or market position.

High value, high risk, high impact.

### Characteristics
- Executive visibility  
- Very high value  
- Long lifecycle  
- Strategic importance  
- Changes the business  

### Governance Level
Maximum

### Gate Rigor
Executive Controlled

### Controls
- Executive sponsorship  
- Formal governance boards  
- Full commercial modelling  
- Strategic risk management  
- Delivery and capability validation  

### Review Cadence
Weekly + Monthly + Executive governance reviews

---

# Governance Summary

| Classification | Governance Level | Gate Rigor | Review Cadence |
|----------------|----------------|------------|----------------|
| Lite | Low | Light | Ad hoc / Weekly |
| Standard | Medium | Structured | Weekly |
| Strategic | High | Strong | Weekly + Monthly |
| Complex | Very High | Rigorous | Weekly + Monthly + Escalation |
| Transformational | Maximum | Executive Controlled | Weekly + Monthly + Executive |

---

# Core Principle

Classification determines:

- Level of governance  
- Depth of controls  
- Frequency of review  
- Evidence required to progress  

---

# Simple Summary

Lite = repeatable  
Standard = core business  
Strategic = important  
Complex = difficult  
Transformational = game changing  

---

# Final Thought

The objective is not to make every opportunity complex.

It is to apply the right level of discipline to the right opportunity.

Too little governance creates risk.  
Too much governance slows growth.

Summit applies the right balance.
