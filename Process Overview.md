# Summit
## The Gated Business Lifecycle

The Summit lifecycle defines how organisations identify, win, deliver, and scale revenue.

It aligns sales, delivery, and leadership through clear stages and enforced gates.

---

## Stage 0 — Signal

**Purpose**  
Identify real opportunities before the market fully forms  

**Focus**
- Detect intent early  
- Prioritise high-value signals  
- Engage the right stakeholders  

**Key Activities**
- Account insight and targeting  
- Market triggers and events  
- Relationship activation  
- Early conversations  

**Gate Criteria**
- Clear signal or trigger identified  
- Initial access to stakeholders  
- Strategic fit confirmed  

**Output**
- Opportunity identified  
- Entry into lifecycle  

---

## Stage 1 — Qualify

**Purpose**  
Ensure only winnable, valuable opportunities progress  

**Focus**
- Truth over optimism  
- Quality over volume  
- Early disqualification  

**Key Activities**
- Problem validation  
- Stakeholder mapping  
- Commercial viability check  
- Quick qualification  

**Gate Criteria**
- Defined customer problem  
- Identified decision-maker  
- Budget or value confirmed  
- Realistic path to win  

**Output**
- Qualified opportunity  
- Approved to progress  

---

## Stage 2 — Value

**Purpose**  
Define the commercial impact and shape the solution  

**Focus**
- Outcome over activity  
- Value clarity  
- Alignment with customer priorities  

**Key Activities**
- Value proposition development  
- Cost and delivery modelling  
- Risk assessment  
- Commercial roadmap creation  

**Gate Criteria**
- Clear and quantified value case  
- Solution aligned to need  
- Delivery approach defined  
- Internal commitment secured  

**Output**
- Developed opportunity  
- Go / No-Go decision  

---

## Stage 3 — Convert

**Purpose**  
Convert opportunity into contracted revenue  

**Focus**
- Control over the deal  
- Clear progression  
- Commercial alignment  

**Key Activities**
- Proposal or tender submission  
- Commercial negotiation  
- Legal and contractual alignment  
- Closing plan execution  

**Gate Criteria**
- Offer accepted  
- Commercial agreement reached  
- Contract signed  

**Output**
- Closed Won or Closed Lost  
- Transition to delivery  

---

## Stage 4 — Deliver

**Purpose**  
Deliver value and expand the relationship  

**Focus**
- Execution excellence  
- Value realisation  
- Opportunity expansion  

**Key Activities**
- Mobilisation and onboarding  
- Delivery execution  
- KPI and performance tracking  
- Change management and optimisation  
- Identification of new opportunities  

**Gate Criteria**
- Value delivered against expectations  
- Customer engagement maintained  
- Expansion opportunities identified  

**Output**
- Active delivery  
- Expansion pipeline  

---

## Stage 5 — Amplify

**Purpose**  
Turn successful delivery into scalable growth  

**Focus**
- Advocacy  
- Repeatability  
- Growth acceleration  

**Key Activities**
- Customer satisfaction capture  
- Case study development  
- Reference and advocacy enablement  
- Account expansion and cross-sell  

**Gate Criteria**
- Customer is referenceable  
- Measurable value demonstrated  
- Repeat or expansion business secured  

**Output**
- Advocacy established  
- Repeatable growth engine  

---

## The Principle

If an opportunity does not meet the gate criteria  
it does not progress  

---

## Lifecycle Flow

Signal → Qualify → Value → Convert → Grow → Amplify  

Each stage introduces clarity  
Each gate enforces discipline  
Together they create predictable growth  

---

## What This Enables

- Stronger pipeline quality  
- Better decision-making  
- Alignment across teams  
- More predictable revenue  
- Scalable growth
