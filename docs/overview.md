# Summit Overview

Summit is a practical commercial operating model for sales, business development, account growth, and revenue execution.

It is built around a simple principle:

Not every opportunity should progress, and not every piece of information is needed at the same time.

The model uses stages and gates to ensure that opportunities move forward only when the right evidence, alignment, and commercial logic exist.

## The Six Stages

### Stage 0 Signal
An initial signal appears in the market, an account, a network, or an inbound enquiry.

### Stage 1 Qualify
The opportunity is tested to confirm whether it is real, valuable, winnable, and deliverable.

### Stage 2 Value
The business case is shaped. The customer problem, commercial value, solution logic, and strategic fit are developed.

### Stage 3 Convert
The opportunity is advanced to a buying decision through stakeholder alignment, proposal quality, risk handling, and commercial agreement.

### Stage 4 Deliver
The commitment is translated into successful mobilisation and delivery readiness.

### Stage 5 Amplify
The delivered work is expanded into reference value, renewals, upsell, cross sell, advocacy, and strategic growth.

## The Role of Gates

Each stage ends with a gate review.

The gate is the moment where the team decides one of four things:

- Progress
- Hold
- Rework
- Stop

This improves forecasting accuracy, sales discipline, and resource allocation.

## CRM Design Principle

The CRM should reflect the commercial journey.

That means:

- Stage aligned fields
- Clear mandatory data
- Decision evidence not just activity logs
- Strong handover between sales and delivery
- Useful reporting for leadership
