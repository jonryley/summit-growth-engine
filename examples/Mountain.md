# Summit Example  
## Walking a Mountain

Each stage creates clarity  
Each gate enforces discipline  
Together they get you to the summit safely  

---

## Stage 0 — Signal

**Objective:** Identify the mountain and the intent to climb  

**Activities**
- Choose the mountain (e.g. Scafell Pike, Ben Nevis)  
- Check weather patterns and conditions  
- Assess personal motivation and purpose  
- Initial route awareness  

**Output**
- Clear intent to climb  
- Mountain selected  
- Awareness of conditions  

---

## Stage 1 — Qualify

**Objective:** Decide if the climb should happen  

**Key Questions**
- Is it safe?  
- Is it achievable?  
- Am I prepared?  
- Do I have the right kit?  

**Activities**
- Weather check (final decision window)  
- Route planning and timing  
- Kit check (boots, layers, navigation, nutrition)  
- Inform others of plan  

**Gate Criteria**
- Weather within safe limits  
- Route understood  
- Kit complete  
- Physical readiness confirmed  

**Output**
- Go / No-Go decision  
- Approved to start ascent  

---

## Stage 2 — Value

**Objective:** Plan the climb properly to maximise success  

**Activities**
- Break route into stages  
- Identify checkpoints and rest points  
- Plan pacing and energy use  
- Assess risks (terrain, exposure, navigation points)  

**Gate Criteria**
- Route plan clear and realistic  
- Risks understood and mitigated  
- Energy and timing aligned  

**Output**
- Structured ascent plan  
- Confidence in execution  

---

## Stage 3 — Convert

**Objective:** Execute the ascent to reach the summit  

**Activities**
- Begin ascent with discipline  
- Maintain pace and hydration  
- Navigate accurately  
- Adjust to conditions in real time  

**Gate Criteria**
- Route maintained  
- Energy levels sustainable  
- Conditions remain within tolerance  

**Output**
- Summit reached  
- Or decision taken to turn back safely  

---

## Stage 4 — Deliver

**Objective:** Descend safely and consolidate the experience  

**Activities**
- Controlled descent  
- Maintain focus (most accidents happen here)  
- Support team members if in group  
- Reflect on performance  

**Gate Criteria**
- Safe return maintained  
- Team accounted for  
- No unnecessary risk taken  

**Output**
- Safe completion of climb  
- Experience gained  

---

## Stage 5 — Amplify

**Objective:** Turn the climb into something bigger  

**Activities**
- Share the story  
- Capture lessons learned  
- Inspire others  
- Build towards the next challenge  

**Gate Criteria**
- Experience reflected on  
- Value captured (personal or collective)  
- Story worth sharing  

**Output**
- Growth beyond the climb  
- Momentum for the next ascent  

---

## The Reality

Not every climb reaches the summit  

And that is the point  

Discipline is knowing when to continue  
and when to turn back  

---

## The Outcome

- Safe, repeatable mountain ascents  
- Increased capability and confidence  
- Stronger mindset and resilience  
- A journey that scales beyond a single climb  

---

## For the Light

Every step forward matters  
Every decision matters  

The summit is earned  
not given
