# Gate 4 Delivery and Value Realisation Gate

## Purpose

Decide whether the customer outcome has been successfully delivered and whether the relationship is ready for renewal, expansion, or amplification.

## Gate Criteria

- Contracted outcomes have been delivered
- Customer success measures have been achieved or are on track
- Commercial performance is acceptable
- Delivery risks and issues are controlled
- Customer relationship health is positive
- Governance and reporting obligations are complete
- Lessons learned and operational insight have been captured
- Follow-on growth or expansion opportunities are identified

## Governance Questions

- Did we deliver the promised outcome?
- Is the customer achieving measurable value?
- Was delivery commercially successful?
- Are customer stakeholders satisfied and aligned?
- Have risks, issues, and obligations been properly closed?
- Is the relationship positioned for future growth?
- What operational or commercial lessons have been learned?

## Decision Options

- Progress to Stage 5 Amplify
- Hold pending remediation or stabilisation
- Rework delivery or customer success plan
- Close and exit

## Gate Outcome

- Successful engagements progress into renewal, expansion, advocacy, or amplification activity
- Held engagements remain under operational review
- Rework engagements require corrective action or service recovery
- Closed engagements are formally completed and archived

## Leadership Test

This is where reputation, trust, and long-term growth are earned.

Strong organisations do not measure success purely by contract signature.

They measure success by delivered outcomes, customer confidence, operational excellence, and the ability to create long-term strategic growth.
