# Gate 5 Amplification and Growth Gate

## Purpose

Decide whether the customer relationship, delivery success, and commercial position are strong enough to justify strategic growth, expansion, and long-term partnership investment.

## Gate Criteria

- Customer outcomes and value have been demonstrated
- Relationship trust and stakeholder alignment are strong
- Renewal, expansion, or strategic growth opportunities exist
- Customer advocacy potential is credible
- Commercial performance supports continued investment
- Operational capability exists to scale successfully
- Lessons learned and best practices have been captured
- Growth opportunities align to strategic objectives

## Governance Questions

- Has measurable customer value been achieved?
- Is the relationship strategically strong?
- Are there credible expansion or renewal opportunities?
- Can this relationship create long-term growth?
- Is the customer willing to advocate for us?
- Can operational success be repeated or scaled?
- Does continued investment align to strategy and resource priorities?

## Decision Options

- Expand and invest
- Maintain and optimise
- Hold pending further opportunity development
- Exit or reduce investment

## Gate Outcome

- Strategic accounts progress into long-term growth and partnership activity
- Stable accounts remain under active account management
- Held accounts remain under observation pending future opportunity
- Exit accounts are managed through controlled disengagement or completion

## Leadership Test

This is where organisations move from transactional selling to strategic growth.

Strong organisations amplify trust, outcomes, relationships, insight, and operational excellence into repeatable long-term growth.

The goal is not simply to win work.

The goal is to create enduring customer value, market credibility, and scalable commercial momentum.
