# Rail Sector Overview

The rail industry is one of the most operationally complex and governance-heavy sectors in the UK.

Winning business in rail is not just about selling.

It is about:

- Safety
- Governance
- Assurance
- Compliance
- Delivery credibility
- Operational readiness
- Framework positioning
- Stakeholder navigation
- Long-term relationship trust

Rail buyers typically assess:

- Can you operate safely?
- Can you work on the railway?
- Can you deliver without disruption?
- Can you integrate with operational teams?
- Can you comply with standards?
- Can you scale nationally?
- Can you manage risk and assurance?

This means rail opportunities require a far more structured commercial lifecycle than standard enterprise sales.

# Rail-Specific Requirements

## Safety Critical Environment

Rail is safety-first.

Commercial teams must understand:

- Safe systems of work
- Possession planning

- Access constraints
- Site assurance
- Competency compliance

Without this understanding, credibility is lost quickly.

---

# Site Access Requirements

## Sentinel Cards

Most operational rail work requires:

- Sentinel Scheme sponsorship
- Valid Sentinel card
- Verified competencies
- Medical compliance

Common Sentinel competencies include:

- PTS (Personal Track Safety)
- IWA (Industry Welfare Access)
- COSS
- Lookout
- Site Warden

---

# PTS — Personal Track Safety

PTS is often the minimum requirement for trackside access.

It confirms individuals understand:

- Rail hazards
- Safe access rules
- Emergency procedures
- Electrified line risks
- Track safety protocols

Without PTS, access to many operational rail sites is restricted.

---

# RISQS

RISQS is a major supplier assurance framework within UK rail.

It validates:

- Safety capability
- Operational processes
- Financial standing
- Technical competence
- Audit readiness

Many rail procurement processes require RISQS alignment before engagement.
